# Code of Conduct

---

## Our Commitment
- We are committed to providing a welcoming, professional, collaborative, and learning-focused environment for all participants.
- This community exists to:
- - Learn together
- - Build useful software
- - Support research and innovation
- - Share knowledge openly
- - Help students gain real-world engineering experience
- Every participant is expected to contribute positively to the community.

---

## Our Values

### Continuous Learning
- Learning is one of the primary goals of this program.
- Participants are encouraged to:
- - Ask questions
- - Seek feedback
- - Explore new technologies
- - Share knowledge with others

---

### Professionalism
- We aim to follow industry-standard software engineering practices.
- Participants should:
- - Be responsible
- - Meet commitments
- - Communicate clearly
- - Respect project processes

---

### Collaboration
- Software is built by teams.
- Participants are expected to:
- - Work respectfully with others
- - Support fellow contributors
- - Share knowledge openly
- - Accept constructive feedback

---

### Integrity
- We value honesty and ethical behavior.
- Participants should:
- - Give proper credit for contributions
- - Avoid plagiarism
- - Report issues honestly
- - Respect intellectual property rights

---

## Expected Behavior
- Examples of positive behavior include:
- - Being respectful and courteous
- - Helping new contributors
- - Providing constructive feedback
- - Being open to learning
- - Taking responsibility for mistakes
- - Communicating professionally
- - Following project guidelines
- - Respecting deadlines and commitments

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

### Harassment
- Including but not limited to:
- - Personal attacks
- - Insults
- - Intimidation
- - Threats
- - Bullying

---

### Discrimination
- Discrimination based on any personal characteristic is not permitted.
- All contributors should be treated fairly and respectfully.

---

### Academic Misconduct
- Examples include:
- - Plagiarism
- - Misrepresenting work completed by others
- - Submitting copied code without attribution
- - Claiming contributions that were not made

---

### Unprofessional Conduct
- Examples include:
- - Repeatedly ignoring mentor guidance
- - Deliberately disrupting project activities
- - Spamming communication channels
- - Misusing project resources
- - Repeated failure to follow contribution processes

---

### Security Violations
- Participants must not:
- - Share credentials
- - Access systems without authorization
- - Attempt to bypass security controls
- - Introduce malicious code
- - Expose sensitive information
- Security concerns should be reported immediately.

---

## Communication Guidelines
- We encourage:
- - Respectful discussions
- - Constructive technical debates
- - Clear written communication
- - Professional language
- When providing feedback:
- - Focus on the work, not the person
- - Explain suggestions clearly
- - Be respectful and objective

---

## Open Source Etiquette
- Contributors should:
- - Review existing documentation before asking questions
- - Search existing issues before creating new ones
- - Respect reviewer feedback
- - Follow project standards
- - Keep discussions professional

---

## Mentor Responsibilities
- Mentors are expected to:
- - Support learning
- - Provide constructive feedback
- - Encourage participation
- - Maintain a respectful environment
- - Guide contributors toward professional practices

---

## Student Responsibilities
- Students are expected to:
- - Participate actively
- - Complete assigned learning activities
- - Follow project processes
- - Communicate blockers early
- - Seek help when needed
- - Continue improving technical and professional skills

---

## Conflict Resolution
- When disagreements occur:
- - Discuss the issue respectfully
- - Focus on facts and technical considerations
- - Seek mentor guidance when necessary
- - Work toward a constructive resolution
- The goal is collaboration, not winning arguments.

---

## Reporting Concerns
- If you experience or observe behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:
- - Document the concern
- - Report it to a mentor or program coordinator
- - Provide relevant details and evidence where possible
- Reports will be reviewed fairly and confidentially whenever practical.

---

## Enforcement
- Project maintainers and mentors may take appropriate action when violations occur.
- Possible actions include:
- - Informal guidance
- - Warning
- - Temporary restriction of project activities
- - Removal from specific projects
- - Removal from the program
- Actions will be based on the severity and frequency of violations.

---

## Recognition of Positive Contributions
- We value contributors who:
- - Help others learn
- - Share knowledge
- - Demonstrate leadership
- - Follow engineering best practices
- - Improve project quality
- - Strengthen the community
- Positive contributions help build a stronger learning environment for everyone.

---

## Final Principle
- We are building more than software.
- We are building a community where students, researchers, engineers, and contributors learn together, create together, and grow together through professional collaboration and open-source development.
